▶

"Look up at the stars and not down at your feet. Try to make sense of what you see, and wonder about what makes the universe exist. Be curious."

Biography

Stephen William Hawking was a world-renowned theoretical physicist, cosmologist, and author. He made groundbreaking contributions to the fields of cosmology, general relativity, and quantum gravity, particularly in the context of black holes. Despite being diagnosed with a rare form of motor neurone disease (ALS) at the age of 21, which left him almost completely paralyzed, Hawking continued to work and inspire millions through his research and writings. His best-selling book, A Brief History of Time, brought complex scientific concepts to the public in an accessible way. His perseverance, intellect, and curiosity about the universe made him one of the most admired scientists in history.
